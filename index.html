Meet Tessa

I understand what it means to live between worlds.

I’m Tessa van der Meiden, the psychologist behind Balance & Connect. I have six years of experience supporting teenagers and adults across international settings.

I was born abroad to an Irish mother and Dutch father. My childhood took me from Romania to Peru, Gabon, Egypt, and the Netherlands, with later chapters in South Africa and California. After qualifying as a psychologist in 2020, I counselled teenagers for two years at an international school in Beijing, followed by three years in Bangkok.

These experiences taught me that international life can be both enriching and complex. We may carry different versions of ourselves across countries, cultures, and languages. In our work, every part of you and your story is welcome.

My counselling approach

Integrative, practical, and centred on you.

Challenges are not always signs that something is wrong with us. They can be signals that something in our lives needs attention. Together, we look at what feels out of balance, reconnect with what matters to you, and identify a helpful way forward.

Counselling may include elements of:

  • Client-centred therapy
  • Solution-focused therapy
  • Cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT)
  • Trauma-focused CBT
  • Acceptance and commitment therapy (ACT)
  • Practical emotion-regulation strategies
  • Psychoeducation

For expats & international families

You do not have to explain the international part.

Living internationally can bring opportunity, adventure, and connection. It can also leave you feeling split between places, missing a former life, or unsure where you fully belong.

As an English-speaking psychologist in the Netherlands with my own third-culture background, I offer therapy for expats where the international context is already understood. We can explore the practical and emotional realities of life abroad while keeping sight of who you are beneath the move.

Teenagers in international families may be adapting to a new language, school, culture, or version of themselves while parents navigate their own transition. My experience in international schools helps me support both the young person’s confidential space and the wider systems around them.

What to expect

A simple, unhurried beginning.

Reaching out can feel like a big step. The first conversations are designed to be clear, reassuring, and without pressure. You will speak with me directly at every stage.

  1. 1

    Free introduction

    A free 15-minute telephone or video call to hear what brings you here, answer practical questions, and see whether working together feels like a good match for you or your teenager.

  2. 2

    First consultation

    In our first online session, we take time to understand your situation, wider context, and hopes for counselling. For a teenager, we agree how parents or school may be involved when appropriate.

  3. 3

    Ongoing support

    If we decide to continue, we agree on a way of working that suits you or your teenager. I remain your direct point of contact, and we review the process together as we go.

No GP referral is needed to begin.

Insurance & referral

Independent care

Having recently returned to the Netherlands, I currently work outside the insured Dutch mental healthcare system (GGZ). Sessions are not covered by Dutch health insurance, and you do not need a referral from your GP.

International access

Flexible where possible

A sliding-scale fee may be available for international students or clients living outside the Netherlands, based on personal circumstances and local cost of living.

Time & payment

Clinical telephone or video consultations and substantive email responses count towards total treatment time. Making or rescheduling an appointment does not carry a direct cost.

International clients may pay by international bank transfer, where extra transfer costs can apply, or by PayPal.

Scope of support

Finding the right level of care

This service supports teenagers and adults with concerns such as anxiety, stress, burnout, belonging, grief, difficult experiences, and life transitions.

Online counselling here is not the right level of care for someone in acute distress, experiencing active suicidal thoughts with intent or a plan, or needing intensive medical or psychiatric support. Some concerns may also benefit from a more specialised service.

If I am not the right person to help directly, I will do my best to help you identify and connect with a service that better fits your needs.

Need urgent help?

My practice and this website do not provide emergency or crisis support. If you or someone else is in immediate danger, call 112. For urgent mental health support, contact your GP or out-of-hours GP service (huisartsenpost). If you are thinking about suicide, contact 113 Suicide Prevention or call 113 free of charge from within the Netherlands.

Concerned about a young person?

For advice about child safety, contact Veilig Thuis free of charge on 0800-2000. The service is available 24/7, and you can discuss a situation anonymously.
