Families

For expats & international families

When life abroad feels more complicated than expected.

Starting over in a new country asks a great deal of us. Even a welcome move can bring grief, disorientation, relationship strain, or a quiet feeling of being out of place.

As an English-speaking psychologist in the Netherlands, I offer therapy for expats in the Netherlands who want a space where the international context does not need to be explained away. We can explore the personal and practical realities of relocation while keeping sight of the person beneath the move.

For international families in the Netherlands, transitions can be layered. Children may be adapting to a new language or school while parents manage their own uncertainty. A child psychologist for expat families can help make room for each family member’s experience and support more open communication.

What to expect

A simple, unhurried beginning.

Reaching out can feel like a big step. The first conversations are designed to be clear, reassuring, and without pressure.

  1. 1

    Introductory call

    A brief, confidential call to hear what brings you here, answer practical questions, and consider whether working together feels like a good fit.

  2. 2

    First consultation

    We take time to understand your current situation, wider context, and hopes for support. For children, we discuss the most suitable way to begin.

  3. 3

    Ongoing support

    If we decide to continue, we agree on a way of working that suits your needs. We review the process together as we go.
